MIMOSA

Ingredients

  • 2 oz. orange juice
  • 4 oz. Brut champagne

Optional

  • 1 tbsp. Grand Marnier
  • dash orange bitters

Directions

  • Fill champagne flute 2/3 full of fresh-squeezed orange juice.
  • Top up with brut champagne.
  • Add the teaspoon of Grand Marnier.

And if you're looking for something with a slightly more tropical flavor, there's also the Blushing Mimosa. For that one, create a mixture that's two parts orange juice, one part pineapple juice. Fill the champagne flute 2/3 full of the juice mixture, top with Champagne, and then add two tablespoons of grenadine.

MOJITO

Ingredients

  • 1/2 oz. lime juice (squeezed fresh)
  • 1 tsp. superfine sugar
  • 3 mint leaves
  • 2 oz. white rum
  • Club soda or seltzer
  • Collins glass

Directions

  • In a smallish Collins glass, muddle lime juice with 1/2 to 1 teaspoon superfine sugar
  • Add the few mint leaves, mushing them against the side of the glass
  • Fill glass 2/3 with cracked ice and pour in the rum
  • Pitch in the squeezed-out lime shell and top off with club soda or seltzer

PIÑA COLADA

Ingredients

  • 2 1/2 oz. rum
  • 3 oz. pineapple juice
  • 1 oz. coconut cream
  • Collins glass

Directions

  • Combine the rum (Puerto Rican, Dominican, or Cuban are best–truth be told, we prefer ours with a more romantic-tasting añejo, such as Brugal or the Bacardi 8), unsweetened pineapple juice (you can sub in 3 ounces crushed—or whole—pineapple), and coconut cream (not coconut juice or milk; you want the sweetened stuff with the chemicals—Coco Lopez is traditional) in a blender.
  • Blend on high with a cup or so of crushed ice or 5 or 6 ice cubes (add them 1 or 2 at a time, through the hole in the lid)
  • Pour into tall glass. Garnish with—hell, whatever you've got. Cherry. Pineapple. Fruit. For extra eye-appeal, pour 1/2 ounce Cherry Heering in over the back of a spoon and watch it swirl.
